## The Challenge
As a brand just starting out, The Boarding House Skateshop needed to build its entire presence from the ground up. They required a cohesive visual identity that captured the modern yet classic look of skate culture, alongside their very first website to act as a digital catalog for their merch. Because they were a grassroots, local startup, they lacked pre-existing brand assets and wanted to keep their startup costs as low as possible.

My challenge was to take full ownership of this blank canvas, establishing their foundational brand guidelines from scratch, designing a rich web layout in Photoshop, and coding it entirely within Tumblr's free, blogging-centric platform. The goal was to launch the brand with a highly interactive, responsive space that showcased their products and connected deeply with the local skate community.

<br />

## My Approach & Implementation
Since I was responsible for the entire visual and technical development for this new brand, I used my creative background to build a comprehensive brand guideline and visual identity that felt both modern and classic.

With the new visual assets locked in, I designed the web design in Photoshop. I then sliced the assets and built a fully custom, responsive frontend theme directly inside Tumblr, implementing several specialized content modules:

**The Visual Catalog Layout**<br />
Structured a clean, intuitive homepage flow featuring a hero banner, a dedicated featured products showcase, and an organized footer. Because the products were purely for show, I focused on a simple product presentation to drive in-store desire.

**The Team Rider Carousel**<br />
Coded a responsive media slider to showcase the shop’s skate team riders, reinforcing the brand's local community roots and authentic lifestyle edge.

**Social & Location Integration**<br />
Embedded a live social media feed for Twitter and Instagram alongside a Google Map render of the physical shop's location to bridge the digital experience with the physical world, actively driving foot traffic to their physical brick-and-mortar location.

As always, during the engineering phase, I utilized Foundation to ensure the entire multi-component layout was fully responsive, and cross-browser compatible.

<br />

## Results & Impact
**End-to-End Brand Launch**<br />
Successfully established a complete, recognizable brand identity and digital presence for a business just starting out, handling everything from logo design to frontend deployment.

**High-Impact, Zero-Overhead Hub**<br />
Enabled a small local business to run a highly polished online catalog and community site with completely zero monthly hosting costs or platform maintenance fees.

**Community-Centric Web Experience**<br />
Delivered a modern, classic layout that perfectly balanced product showcasing with authentic skate culture elements, smoothly scaling across all screen sizes and modern web browsers.
